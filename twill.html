Twill Fabric

Twill is a strong, durable woven fabric known for its distinctive diagonal rib pattern, created through a specific weaving technique where the weft yarn passes over one or more warp yarns. This tight weave gives twill fabrics excellent strength, drape, and wrinkle resistance, making them ideal for both fashion and utility garments. Twill is commonly made from cotton, polyester, wool, or blends, and is available in various weights and finishes. With a smooth surface on one side and a slightly textured feel on the other, twill fabric is opaque, resistant to wear, and tends to show fewer stains and creases than plain-weave fabrics. Popular types of twill include denim, chino, drill, and gabardine, each suited to specific applications. Due to its durability and refined appearance, twill is widely used in pants, jackets, uniforms, skirts, suits, upholstery, and home textiles.
